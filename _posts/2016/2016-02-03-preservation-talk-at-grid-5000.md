Prof. Thain gave a talk titled
  <a href="http://www.nd.edu/~dthain/talks/preservation-grid5k.pdf">
   Preservation and Portability in Distributed Scientific Applications
  </a>
  at the
  <a href="http://www.grid5000.org/">
   Grid-5000 Winter School
  </a>
  on distributed computing in Grenoble, France.  I gave a broad overview of our recent efforts, including
  <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/MCSE.2015.111">
   distributing software
  </a>
  with Parrot and CVMFS,
  <a href="http://ccl.cse.nd.edu/research/papers/tauroast-casestudy-jpcs2015.pdf">
   preserving
  </a>
  high energy physics applications with Parrot, specifying environments with
  <a href="http://ccl.cse.nd.edu/software/umbrella">
   Umbrella
  </a>
  , and preserving workflows with
  <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.7274/R0CZ353M">
   PRUNE
  </a>
  .
